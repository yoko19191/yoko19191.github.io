“common case : ”
格式：PDF, slide

注意：

  1. 体积小(1-2M)
  2. 字体大小，方便手机预览
  3. 21页以内
  4. 做不同版本的演讲，30m, 15m, 5m, 电梯演讲
  5. 投资人心态：关注增长， fear of missing out
  6. 假设, 看的人非常没有耐心
  7. 写BP没有定式

内容：

  1. 团队介绍

  2. 项目在做什么

  3. 有什么样的市场需求， 市场有多大

  4. 收入构成， 收入预测

  5. 项目为什么能做成， 核心优势在哪里？

  6. 相关数据

  7. 和竞争对手的关系

  8. 财务情况

  9. 路线图

  10. 股权结构， 准备融多少钱

  11. 机会和切入点

  12. 未来会做成什么样

  13. 创业的动机和打法风格

  14. 团队是否擅长

Something about pitching

  • Crafting a compelling product pitch can be a difficult process for those who have a technical, engineering, or non-sales background, or another non-sales discipline.
  • A pitch can go awry when the presenter gets too wrapped up in details rather than concentrating on the central idea, or has not thought through the idea enough to really understand it.
  • An audience will give presenters 60 seconds to capture their attention—then they tune out.
  • A key for entrepreneurs pitching their plans is to show passion for the idea and for the audience. "An idea isn't well understood until it can be explained to someone else. " (Thomas Steenburgh, Harvard Business School)

About the pitch

  • Purpose – why making a pitch
    • Get the audience interested to hear and see more about your project -> next meeting -> deal
  • Audience – know your audience in advance
    • Technical or non-technical?
    • Investor, customer, potential employer, business partner
  • Duration
    • 60 sec, 5 mins, 10 mins, find out in advance the time limit specific to the event
  • Pitch vs. product demo
    • Pitch is not a demo, but may allow you to give a demo later

Timing and tools

  1. 60 second pitch § No slides really § Poster may be used as your background
  2. 5 minute pitch § 4-5 slides to support highlights in your talk § Only main bullets on slides § Graphics/high level statistics (“evidence”)
  3. Project demo § Separate from pitch, additional time needed § Show only highlights of your work § Be aware of the risk in loosing your audience in details

Pitch structure (an example)

  • Describe the problem or need you are solving or addressing

    • Give a scale and importance meaningful to a ‘common man’

    • Quantify if possible (“Market data”)

  • Tell, what your solution provides for its user

  • Convince the audience on the power of your solution with facts

  • Highlight your team and its skills

    • Give reasons why exactly your team is capable of solving this problem

Some tips

  • Use a reference to phenomenon or event in common life (if
    applicable)
  • Personalize your message (if applicable) and show your
    personal interest and motivation
  • Do not get lost or let your audience get lost by too much of
    details
  • Focus, take it seriously
  • Prepare, practice, practice, practice, watch your time
